Research

I have been involved in research throughout my life. In high school I took part in 2 research programs aimed at high school students. The first was through an organization at UCLA called LAPTAG which allowed high schoolers to experience the research lab environment. This research was on Plasma Physics, UCLA has several plasma machines where research on plasma is conducted. One of them is dedicated to high schoolers.

The other program through Caltech was about Cosmic Rays, it was called CHICOS. CHICOS put Cosmic Ray Detectors in high schools throughout the San Fernando and San Gabriel Valleys and collected the data. They would run programs for high schoolers to build the detectors and analyze the data. Using existing programs and collected data we were able to make educated guesses at the initial particle energy and the “shape” of the cosmic ray shower. The goal was to eventually guess where the highest energy initial particles were coming from, and whether they could have come from various high energy cosmic events, such as gamma ray bursts or active galactic nuclei.

The summer before my senior year I took my Taglit Birthright trip. Since almost my entire extended family is in Israel, I knew I would extend my trip. I decided that this was a good opportunity to get some more research experience so I found a lab to work in for a month in the summer. The lab was at the Israel Institute of Technology (Technion) and the research was on the Josephson Effect in Bose-Einstein Condensates. The research involved hands on work with lasers and optics, data analysis in Excel, some work in Matlab, and image analysis. I learned about Evaporative Cooling, Laser Cooling, Magnetic Traps, and various imaging techniques.

After getting back from that summer in Israel, I joked that with my previous research in Plasma Physics, I was now an expert on all 5 states of matter. While I still love and enjoy physics, my path in life has turned away from it. However, the skills I learned while studying physics and working the lab have proved to be useful in other areas. The curiosity, experimentation, and search for knowledge has aided me in every project and at every position I have held.
